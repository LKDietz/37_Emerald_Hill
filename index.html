About This Study

The 37 Emerald Hill project documents the lived experience of women in this campus through walking interviews and a co-drawing workshop. The research challenges conventional urban planning's reliance on quantitative data — density, GFA, commercial value — by making emotional, social, and historical memory legible as design evidence.

The result is a layered understanding of why this place matters — not just to its alumni, but to the city.

Analytical Framework

Physical Form

How participants describe architectural qualities — scale, material, proportion, spatial sequence — and how these shaped behaviour and memory.

Social Occupation

Communities, hierarchies, rituals, and the school's relationship to its surrounding neighbourhood.

Emotional Densities

Feelings, attachments, fears, and the affective charge of specific spaces — the data most often excluded from urban research.
